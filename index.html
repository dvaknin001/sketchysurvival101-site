We check the claim before we repeat it.

Every number on this site traces back to a real source — the U.S. Department of Energy, the Florida Solar Energy Center, ENERGY STAR, peer-reviewed studies, and original patents.

"A $30 gadget cools your whole house" makes a great headline. Usually the truth is more useful: it does one specific thing, the savings are real but bounded, and there's a right and wrong way to install it. We explain the mechanism, show the math, and link the source so you can verify it yourself.

How we research
Sourced, not guessed. Field-measured figures with citations, not roofing-blog rumor.
